Holland Lop

ABOUT

Holland Lop is a breed of rabbit that was recognized by the American Rabbit Breeders Association in 1979 and by the Netherlands' Governing Rabbit Council in 1984. The Holland Lop, with a maximum weight of of4 lb, is one of the smallest lop-eared breeds. Wikipedia Origin: Netherlands Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus

Mini Lop

ABOUT

The Mini Lop is a breed of domestic rabbit that is recognized by the American Rabbit Breeders Association. It is different from the Miniature Lop breed that is recognized by the British Rabbit Council. The Mini Lop [US] and the Miniature Lop [UK] are different from the Dwarf Lop breed that is recognized by the BRC. Wikipedia Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus Origin: Germany Higher classification: Domestic rabbit Rank: Animal breed

Lionhead rabbit

ABOUT

Lionhead is a breed of domestic rabbit recognized by the British Rabbit Council and by the American Rabbit Breeders Association. The Lionhead rabbit has a wool mane encircling the head, reminiscent of a male lion as its name implies. Wikipedia Origin: Belgium Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus

Netherland Dwarf rabbit

ABOUT

The Netherland Dwarf is a breed of domestic rabbit that originated in the Netherlands. Weighing 1.1–2.5 pounds, the Netherland Dwarf is one of the smallest rabbit breeds. Its popularity as a pet or show rabbit may stem from its neotenic appearance. Wikipedia Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus domesticus Lifespan: 8–15 years Rank: Animal breed Higher classification: Domestic rabbit Coat: Short, Fine Weight: 1.1–2.5 pounds (0.50–1.13 kg)

Flemish Giant rabbit

ABOUT

The Flemish Giant rabbit is the largest breed of domestic rabbit. Flemish Giants are historically a utility breed used for their fur and meat. They are often kept as pets as they are known for being docile and patient when being handled. Wikipedia Scientific name: Oryctolagus cuniculus Rank: Animal breed
